Nihari Recipe

Nihari is a slow-cooked stew originating from South Asia, typically made with beef or mutton, simmered overnight with a blend of spices to create a rich and flavorful dish.

Ingredients

  • Beef or mutton
  • Nihari spice mix (including fennel seeds, ginger, garlic, and other spices)
  • Onions
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Wheat flour (for thickening)
  • Ghee or oil
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, green chilies)
  • Ginger slices (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot and saut√© onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the meat and brown it on all sides.
  4. Add the nihari spice mix and salt, stirring to coat the meat evenly.
  5. Add water to cover the meat and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for several hours until the meat is tender.
  6. Mix wheat flour with water to create a slurry and add it to the stew to thicken the gravy. Cook for an additional 30 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs and ginger slices before serving.
  8. Serve hot with naan or rice.
