Bhindi Recipe

Bhindi, or okra, is a popular vegetable in South Asian cuisine, often cooked with a blend of spices to create a flavorful and nutritious dish.

Ingredients

  • Fresh bhindi (okra)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, chili powder)
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry the bhindi thoroughly. Cut off the tops and tails, then slice them into 1-inch pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger, and cook for a couple of minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add the spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, chili powder) and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the sliced bhindi to the pan. Stir to coat the bhindi with the spice mixture.
  7. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the bhindi is tender and cooked through (about 15-20 minutes).
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
  9. Serve hot with roti or rice.

A special tip: To avoid sliminess, make sure the bhindi is completely dry before cooking.

A secret to enhance the flavor is to add meat either chicken, beef, or lamb while cooking.
